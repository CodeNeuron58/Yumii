One real conversation, annotated — so the orb stops being a mystery.

## Reading the orb

| Orb | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Dim, slow pulse | Booting — the backend is loading her models |
| Gentle breathing | Idle — ready and listening for you |
| Expanding rings | Hearing you speak |
| Spinning shimmer | Thinking — your words are with the LLM |
| Pulsing with sound, tinted | Speaking — the color reflects her emotion |

The tint is real: a classifier reads the tone of her reply and colors the
orb to match.

## A first exchange, annotated

**You:** "Hey Yumii, how are you?"

The moment you stop talking, local voice detection closes your utterance and
sends it to speech-to-text. Your words go to the LLM with her personality,
today's date, and everything she remembers about you. Her reply streams to
the voice engine sentence-by-sentence — she starts speaking in about a
second.

**You (mid-sentence):** "Actually — wait."

She stops immediately. This is **barge-in**: the mic stays live while she
speaks, and your voice interrupts hers, like a human conversation.

**You:** "Remember that my sister's birthday is next month."

She saves it on the spot — "remember this" triggers her own memory tool.
Every stored fact is visible and editable in the
[dashboard](/guide/dashboard).

**You:** "What's on the web about the weather tomorrow?"

She reaches for web search — and a prompt appears: *"Allow Yumii to search
the web?"* Nothing happens until you click **Approve**. That gate is the
core of [tools](/guide/tools).

## Mic etiquette

* **Speak naturally** — no wake word, no push-to-talk.
* **Pauses end your turn.** A moment of silence closes your utterance; keep
  the thought flowing, or just continue — interrupting her works fine.
* **A quiet-ish room helps.** Fans and hums are filtered; a loud TV with
  voices can trigger her. Headphones make barge-in crispest.

## When something looks stuck

| Symptom | Cause → fix |
|---|---|
| "Waking Yumii up…" for over a minute | Backend didn't start → [Troubleshooting](/reference/troubleshooting) |
| An error card appears | It names the cause — bad key, rate limit, or network — and what to do |
| She mishears repeatedly | Switch STT to Groq Whisper in [Providers → STT](/providers/stt) |
