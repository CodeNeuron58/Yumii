This page walks through one real conversation and explains what's happening at
each moment, so the orb stops being a mystery.

## Reading the orb

The orb is Yumii's face. Its state tells you exactly where the conversation is:

| Orb | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Dim, slow pulse | Booting — the backend is loading her models |
| Gentle breathing | Idle — she's ready and listening for you |
| Expanding rings | Hearing you speak |
| Spinning shimmer | Thinking — your words are with the LLM |
| Pulsing with sound, tinted | Speaking — the color reflects her emotion |

Her emotional tint is real: a classifier reads the tone of her reply (warm,
teasing, sad, surprised…) and colors the orb to match.

## A first exchange, annotated

**You:** "Hey Yumii, how are you?"

The moment you stop talking (about a third of a second of silence), local
voice-activity detection closes your utterance and sends it to
speech-to-text. Your transcribed words go to the LLM along with her
personality, today's date, and everything she remembers about you. Her reply
streams into the local voice engine sentence-by-sentence — which is why she
starts speaking in about a second instead of waiting to compose the whole
answer.

**You (while she's mid-sentence):** "Actually — wait."

She stops immediately. This is **barge-in**: the microphone stays live while
she speaks, and your voice interrupts hers, exactly like a human conversation.
You never need to wait for her to finish.

**You:** "Remember that my sister's birthday is next month."

After the turn ends, a background pass extracts *"user's sister has a birthday
next month"* into her local memory. It's deduplicated against what she already
knows and will surface naturally in future conversations. You can see and edit
every stored fact in the [dashboard](/guide/dashboard).

**You:** "What's on the web about the weather tomorrow?"

She decides to use her web-search tool — and a small prompt appears on
screen: *"Allow Yumii to search the web?"* Nothing external happens until you
click **Approve**. This gate is the core of how Yumii handles
[tools](/guide/tools).

## Mic etiquette (what works best)

* **Speak naturally.** No wake word, no push-to-talk — if the orb is idle or
  ringing, she's listening.
* **Pauses end your turn.** If you pause mid-thought for more than a moment,
  she'll treat it as your turn ending. Keep the thought flowing, or just
  continue — interrupting her reply works fine.
* **Quiet-ish room helps.** Speech detection filters hums and fans, but a loud
  TV with voices in it can trigger her. Headphones eliminate the echo of her
  own voice entirely (though echo cancellation handles speakers well).

## When something looks stuck

* Orb stays on "Waking Yumii up…" for over a minute → the backend didn't
  start; see [Troubleshooting](/reference/troubleshooting).
* She thinks forever and gives up → almost always the LLM provider's rate
  limit (free tiers have daily caps). Also in
  [Troubleshooting](/reference/troubleshooting).
* She heard you wrong repeatedly → consider switching speech-to-text to Groq
  Whisper (faster, more accurate, cloud) in [Providers → STT](/providers/stt).
