Most AI today is transactional: you open a tab, type a prompt, read an answer,
close the tab. Nothing carries over. Nothing is *present*.

Yumii is an experiment in the opposite direction — an AI that behaves like a
companion rather than a service. She sits on your desktop as a small glowing
orb. You talk to her out loud. She remembers what you told her last week. She
has a personality (six of them, actually). And when she does something in the
real world — reads your inbox, searches the web — she asks you first.

## Companion, not assistant

An assistant answers questions and waits. A companion notices things, follows
up, teases, celebrates. That difference is baked into how Yumii is built:

* **Persistent memory.** After every conversation turn, a background pass
  extracts facts about you ("has a cat named Mochi", "interviewing next
  Tuesday") into a local database. Those facts are woven into her context in
  every future conversation — she references them the way a friend uses
  memory, not the way a database recites rows.
* **Personality as a first-class feature.** Each of the six personas is a
  detailed character — speech patterns, emotional range, how she reacts when
  you're sad, proud, or teasing her. They are plain text files you can read
  and edit.
* **Presence.** The orb pulses while she speaks, shifts color with her mood,
  and stays out of your way. A global hotkey (`Ctrl+Shift+Space`) summons or
  hides her from anywhere.

## Local-first, and what that actually means

"Private" is an easy word to put on a landing page. Here is what it concretely
means in Yumii:

| What | Where it lives |
|---|---|
| Conversations & memory | SQLite files in `~/.yumii/memory/` on your disk |
| API keys | `~/.yumii/auth.json`, an owner-only file — never sent anywhere except the provider it belongs to |
| Her voice | Synthesized locally on your CPU (Kokoro-82M, bundled with the installer) |
| Speech detection | Local (Silero VAD, bundled) |
| The backend | A local server bound to `127.0.0.1` only, with an origin allowlist |

The parts that *do* use the network are the ones you explicitly configure: the
LLM (Groq by default — free tier works), cloud speech-to-text if you choose it
over local Whisper, and any app you connect through Composio. Nothing else
phones home. There is no account, no telemetry, no cloud copy of your life.

## She asks before she acts

Tool-calling AI is powerful and slightly terrifying. Yumii's answer is a
**human-in-the-loop permission gate**: any tool that touches the outside world
(sending email, searching the web) pauses and puts an Approve / Deny prompt on
your screen before it runs. You can relax this per-category or turn it off
entirely — but the default is that *nothing external happens without your
click*. See [Tools & Permissions](/guide/tools).

## What Yumii is not (yet)

Honesty section. As of the current alpha:

* **No animated avatar yet.** The Live2D companion mode is planned; today she
  is an orb. (The voice, memory, and personality are the product — the orb is
  just how she shows up on screen.)
* **She never speaks first.** Proactive check-ins are on the roadmap; today
  she is fully reactive.
* **Windows installer only.** macOS and Linux currently run
  [from source](/development/from-source) — it works, it's just not
  one-click yet.
* **The LLM is (by default) a cloud call.** Her voice and ears can be fully
  local, but the thinking uses a provider API — Groq's free tier by default.
  You can point her at a local model via [Ollama](/providers/llm) if you have
  the hardware.

## The stack, in one paragraph

A Python backend (FastAPI + LangGraph) runs the pipeline: Silero VAD detects
your speech, Whisper transcribes it, a tool-calling LLM agent thinks, a
heuristic synthesizer derives her emotion, and Kokoro synthesizes her voice —
streamed to a Tauri (Rust) desktop shell over a local WebSocket. Every piece
is a pluggable provider. The details live in
[Architecture](/development/architecture).
