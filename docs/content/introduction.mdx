## What is Yumii?

Yumii is an open-source, locally-runnable AI companion. She listens
through your microphone, thinks using a hosted LLM (Groq, OpenAI, or
Anthropic), speaks using a hosted TTS provider (ElevenLabs or
CAMB.ai), and reacts in real time through a Live2D avatar in your
browser.

She is not a chatbot you query. She is a presence that listens,
reacts, and remembers you across sessions — every conversation turn
runs a cheap LLM pass that extracts facts about you and stores them
in local SQLite, and those facts are injected into the system prompt
on every new or resumed session.

## What v0.2.0 ships with

* **Real-time voice conversation** — Silero VAD for speech detection,
  `faster-whisper` (local CPU) or Groq Whisper (cloud) for transcription.
* **Animated Live2D avatar** — open the browser, allow the microphone,
  and Yumii reacts with expressions and body motions driven by the
  LLM's structured output.
* **Six personalities** — `caring`, `tsundere`, `genki`, `kuudere`,
  `yandere`, `dandere`. Each is a plain text file in
  `src/yumii/assets/prompts/`. Add your own.
* **Barge-in** — speak while Yumii is talking and she stops. This is
  the Gemini Live–style feel.
* **Persistent memory** — `~/.yumii/memory/yumii.db` (sessions),
  `store.db` (user facts), and `checkpoints.db` (LangGraph conversation
  history). Survives restarts. CLI: `/chat`, `/resume`, `/sessions`,
  `/memory`, `/forget`, `/name`. See [Memory Architecture](/capabilities/memory-architecture).
* **OS-keychain credentials** — API keys are stored in Windows
  Credential Manager, macOS Keychain, or libsecret. Never in `.env`.
* **Polished CLI** — a Typer + Rich terminal dashboard with a
  pixel-art banner, gradient vision reader, and a guided setup wizard.

## What v0.2.0 does **not** ship with

These are on the roadmap and will land in subsequent releases:

* **A planning / tool-execution agent loop** — v1.0.
* **MCP (Model Context Protocol) server** — v1.0 or v2.0.
* **Multimodal vision input** — post v2.0.
* **A bundled Live2D model** — you bring your own
  (`.model3.json` from any Cubism 4 compatible model). Drop it in
  `~/.yumii/avatar/` and update the `modelUrl` in
  `src/yumii/assets/webui/index.html`.

## Why Yumii?

Because the existing closed-source companions (Replika, Character.AI,
Ava, etc.) require cloud accounts, lock you out of the personality
prompts, and ship voice + avatar as an opaque binary. Yumii is the
opposite: every line of her brain is a text file you can edit, every
key lives in your OS keychain, every STT / TTS / LLM provider is a
pluggable factory, and the avatar runs in your browser using standard
Web tech.

She is not the smartest assistant. She is the one you can actually
read, modify, and own.
