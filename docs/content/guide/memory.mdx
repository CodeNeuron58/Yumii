Yumii has two kinds of remembering, and it helps to keep them straight:

* **Sessions** — the conversations themselves. Word-for-word history, one
  thread per chat, resumable.
* **Facts** — distilled long-term memory about *you*, shared across every
  session and personality.

Both live entirely in local SQLite files under `~/.yumii/memory/`. Nothing is
synced anywhere.

## How facts happen

After every conversation turn, a lightweight background pass reads the
exchange and extracts atomic facts about you — *"has a cat named Mochi"*,
*"prefers short answers in the morning"*, *"interviewing at a startup next
Tuesday"*. Each fact gets a category (identity, preference, habit,
relationship, goal) and a confidence score, and is deduplicated against what
she already knows.

On every turn, her current fact list is included in her context — which is
why she can say "wasn't your interview today?" three days later without you
re-explaining anything.

Two properties worth knowing:

* **Extraction is judgment, not transcription.** She stores things that seem
  durable about you, not a log of everything said. The conversation log
  itself is the session, not the fact store.
* **It's fully inspectable.** Dashboard → **Memory** shows every stored fact.
  If it's not on that list, she doesn't know it.

## Correcting and forgetting

* **Edit a fact** — Dashboard → Memory → Edit. Useful when she misheard
  ("cat named Moshi").
* **Forget one fact** — Dashboard → Memory → Forget.
* **Forget everything** — the CLI command `yumii forget` wipes all facts
  (sessions stay). For a total wipe of *everything* — conversations, facts,
  keys, settings — delete the `~/.yumii` folder while Yumii is closed.

## Sessions in practice

* A new conversation starts when you launch fresh; reconnects and restarts
  keep the same session going rather than fragmenting your history.
* Sessions title themselves from your first sentence and count their
  messages.
* From **Dashboard → Chats** you can read transcripts, resume any past
  session (the orb conversation continues that thread), rename, or delete.

## What's in the files

| File | Contents |
|---|---|
| `~/.yumii/memory/yumii.db` | Session list — titles, timestamps, message counts |
| `~/.yumii/memory/checkpoints.db` | Full conversation history per session (the agent's state) |
| `~/.yumii/memory/store.db` | The fact store |

They're ordinary SQLite files — you can open them with any SQLite browser.
Back up the `memory/` folder and you've backed up your entire relationship
with her; move it to a new machine and she remembers you there.

<Callout type="info" title="Privacy note">
Facts are included in the context sent to your configured LLM provider during
conversation — that's how she uses them. They are never sent anywhere else,
and no other component reads them.
</Callout>
