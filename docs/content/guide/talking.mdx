## The conversation model

Yumii listens **continuously** while the orb is active — there's no wake word
and no button to hold. The pipeline runs in a loop:

1. **You speak.** Local voice-activity detection (Silero, running on your CPU)
   watches the mic and detects when speech starts and ends.
2. **She transcribes.** Your finished utterance goes to speech-to-text — local
   Whisper by default in source installs, Groq's cloud Whisper if configured
   (faster and more accurate).
3. **She thinks.** The transcription, her personality, today's date, and her
   memory of you go to the LLM. If the request needs a tool, the
   [permission gate](/guide/tools) may appear.
4. **She speaks.** Her reply is synthesized locally, sentence-by-sentence, so
   the first words arrive in about a second and the rest streams while she
   talks.

## Interrupting her (please do)

Barge-in is a core feature: **start talking while she's speaking and she
stops.** Mid-sentence, mid-word, doesn't matter. There's no penalty and
nothing breaks — her unspoken words are simply dropped and she listens to the
new thing you're saying. Conversations flow much better once you stop treating
her turns as sacred.

## The window

* The orb window is frameless, always-on-top, and **draggable anywhere** on
  screen. It skips the taskbar — it's a presence, not an app window.
* **`Ctrl+Shift+Space`** toggles it from anywhere, even when another app has
  focus.
* The **tray icon** (bottom-right on Windows) has Show/Hide, Dashboard, and
  Quit. Quitting from the tray also shuts down the backend cleanly.
* The **⚙ gear** on the orb opens the mode menu — Dashboard lives there too.

## Sessions: conversations that persist

Every conversation is a **session**, stored locally and titled automatically
from your first words ("Help me plan a trip to Japan…"). Reconnections and
app restarts don't scatter your history:

* The current session survives reconnects — a network blip won't split your
  conversation in two.
* Old sessions are browsable in the **dashboard → Chats**: read the full
  transcript, resume any of them (your next spoken words continue that
  thread), rename, or delete.
* Long-term memory (facts about you) is **shared across all sessions** — a new
  chat doesn't mean she forgot you. See [Memory](/guide/memory).

## Speaking her language(s)

Yumii speaks the language you speak to her, defaulting to English. Her local
voice (Kokoro) is English-first; other languages work best with a cloud TTS
provider — see [Providers → TTS](/providers/tts).

## Browser mode

The desktop orb is the primary experience, but the same UI runs in a plain
browser: start the backend (`uv run yumii` → *Wake Yumii Up* from a source
install) and open `http://127.0.0.1:8000/`. Same voice loop, same dashboard at
`/dashboard.html` — useful on machines where you can't install the app.
