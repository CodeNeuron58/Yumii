You've [installed Yumii](/get-started/installation). Her first launch sets
itself up — you make exactly one decision: which key powers her thinking.

<StepsList>
  <Step icon="download" title="She downloads her voice and ears">
    On first launch the orb shows a progress bar while her local models
    download — the voice (~330&nbsp;MB) and the ears (~130&nbsp;MB). One-time;
    after this they run offline on your CPU.
  </Step>
  <Step icon="star" title="One key wakes her up">
    A card appears: <em>"I just need a mind to think with."</em> Click
    <strong> Get a free Ollama key</strong> (opens in your browser), paste it,
    and hit <strong>Wake her up</strong>. She restarts herself — no quitting,
    no digging through settings. (Prefer Groq or another provider? "more
    options" opens the dashboard.)
  </Step>
  <Step icon="face-smile" title="Tap the orb and talk">
    Allow microphone access and say hello. She listens continuously — no
    wake word, no push-to-talk. Try interrupting her mid-sentence: she stops
    and listens. That's not a bug, that's the point.
  </Step>
</StepsList>

## Things to try in your first five minutes

* **"Switch to tsundere."** — her whole personality changes on the spot.
* **Tell her something about yourself.** "I've got an interview Friday."
  Ask her about it tomorrow — she'll remember.
* **"Search the web for…"** — a permission prompt appears before she acts.
  That gate guards every external action she ever takes.
* **"What did we talk about yesterday?"** — she searches your past
  conversations and tells you.
* **Press `Ctrl+Shift+Space`** — the hotkey hides and summons the orb from
  anywhere.

## Where everything lives

| Surface | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **The orb** | The conversation — tap to start, talk, interrupt freely. Drag it by grabbing it. |
| **The dashboard** | ⚙ gear or tray → Dashboard: settings, keys, past chats, memory, tools |
| **The tray icon** | Show/hide, dashboard, quit |

<Callout type="info" title="Free tiers have limits">
Ollama's free tier has a weekly quota, Groq's a daily one. Normal
conversation fits comfortably; if she hits a limit she says so with a clear
error card — switch providers in the dashboard or wait for the reset. See
[Troubleshooting](/reference/troubleshooting).
</Callout>
