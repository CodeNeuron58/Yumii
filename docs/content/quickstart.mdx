You've [installed Yumii](/get-started/installation). Everything about her
voice and ears already works locally — the one thing she needs from you is an
API key for her *thinking*. The free [Groq](https://console.groq.com) tier is
the recommended way to start: no card required, and it's fast enough for
real-time conversation.

<StepsList>
  <Step icon="star" title="Get a free Groq API key">
    Sign up at console.groq.com → <strong>API Keys</strong> →
    <strong> Create API Key</strong>. Copy it — it's shown only once.
  </Step>
  <Step icon="layout-dashboard" title="Open the dashboard and paste the key">
    Launch Yumii. When the orb appears, click the <strong>⚙ gear</strong> on
    the orb window (or right-click the tray icon) → <strong>Dashboard</strong>.
    In <strong>Settings → API Keys</strong>, paste your key into
    <strong> Groq API key</strong> and hit <strong>Save settings</strong>.
  </Step>
  <Step icon="play" title="Restart Yumii">
    Quit from the tray icon and launch her again — provider changes load at
    startup. The dashboard tells you whenever a restart is needed.
  </Step>
  <Step icon="face-smile" title="Tap the orb and talk">
    Click the orb, allow microphone access, and say hello. She listens
    continuously — no push-to-talk. Try interrupting her mid-sentence: she
    stops and listens. That's not a bug, that's the point.
  </Step>
</StepsList>

## Things to try in your first five minutes

* **"Switch to tsundere."** — her whole personality changes on the spot.
  There are six: caring (the default), tsundere, genki, kuudere, yandere,
  dandere.
* **Tell her something about yourself.** "I've got a job interview on
  Friday." Then ask her about it tomorrow — she'll remember.
* **"Search the web for…"** — her first tool. A small permission prompt
  appears before she actually searches; that gate guards every external
  action she ever takes.
* **Press `Ctrl+Shift+Space`** — the global hotkey hides and summons the orb
  from anywhere.

## Where everything lives

* The **orb** is the conversation. Tap to start, talk naturally, interrupt
  freely.
* The **dashboard** (gear icon or tray → Dashboard) is the control room:
  settings and keys, past conversations with readable transcripts, connected
  tools, and everything she remembers about you.
* The **tray icon** is lifecycle: show/hide, dashboard, quit.

<Callout type="info" title="On Groq's free tier">
The free tier has daily token quotas and per-request size caps. Normal
conversation fits comfortably; a heavy day of tool use can bump into them —
she'll fail politely when it happens. See the
[Troubleshooting](/reference/troubleshooting) page for what the
limits look like and what your options are.
</Callout>
